BIG-5 PROJECT — NATURE TAXONOMY (REFERENCE)

AXIS: BIOTIC vs. ABIOTIC

BIOTIC — living organisms, or the material/immaterial result of biotic processes,
present in an ecosystem, interacting, adapting, and evolving. Includes:
- Natural or semi-natural flora and derivatives (plants, wild or potted; food like
  sugar, honey, coffee, liqueur)
- Natural or semi-natural fauna and derivatives (wild, domesticated, or bred
  vertebrates, molluscs, insects; derivatives like butter)
- Natural or semi-natural fungi and other organisms (moulds, yeasts, mushrooms,
  bacteria)
- Natural or semi-natural ecosystems (natural reserve, urban park, vegetable garden)

ABIOTIC — non-living elements or processes, material or immaterial, that
characterize or occur in an ecosystem and affect the behavior/survival of organisms.
Includes:
- Hydrological components (river, pond, lake)
- Atmospheric/weather/seasonal components (rain, cloud, snow, wind, temperature,
  winter, summer) — excluding non-natural uses like "summer school"
- Geological components (salt, petrol, oil)
- Astrophysical components/processes (sunset, sunrise, day, night, stars, moon) —
  excluding purely conversational uses like "good morning/night"
