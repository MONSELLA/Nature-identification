MATERIAL — the element has physical presence and can be perceived through the senses
(it occupies space and has mass). Includes:
- Biophysical processes (e.g., photosynthesis, evapotranspiration)
- Meteorological elements/processes (e.g., rain, fog, wind)
- Astrophysical elements/processes (e.g., sunset, sunrise, eclipse, meteor)
- Natural or semi-natural flora (plants, wild or potted, and derivatives like honey,
  coffee)
- Natural or semi-natural fauna (wild, domesticated, or bred vertebrates, molluscs,
  insects, and derivatives like butter)
- Natural or semi-natural fungi (wild or cultivated mushrooms, moulds, yeasts)
- Natural or semi-natural ecosystems (e.g., a natural reserve, urban park) —
  excluding administrative units (country, county, district)
- Edible solid/liquid elements at any processing level (unless only a package with the
  element's name printed on it)
- Nature-based artefacts where the natural material of origin is clearly identifiable
  (e.g., a surface with visible wood grain or stone texture) — excluding artefacts
  with homogenous, unidentifiable texture (e.g., a smooth statue)

IMMATERIAL — concepts, ideas, or fictional/artistic representations of nature that are
depicted or referenced, but are not a realistic, unfiltered depiction (a plain
descriptive photo or text does not count as immaterial — it is material or excluded).
Two subtypes, with illustrative examples of the range of formats this can take
(video games, cartoons, humanized/fake animals or plants, costumes, songs, poems,
drawings, paintings, prints, artistic photos, metaphors, proverbs, virtual reality,
photos of photos, physical geography maps, infographics, AI-generated imagery, plain
illustrations, etc.) — these formats are examples to aid recognition, not a required
output classification:
- Fictional representation: a depiction that reimagines or fabricates a nature
  element in non-literal form (e.g., video games, cartoons, humanized animals, fake
  plants or animals, costumes).
- Art/cultural/scientific representation: a depiction that presents a nature element
  through an artistic, cultural, or informational lens (e.g., songs, poems, drawings,
  paintings, prints, artistic photos, metaphors, proverbs, virtual reality, photos of
  photos, physical geography maps — political/administrative maps excluded).
